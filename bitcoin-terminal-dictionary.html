SECTION A - ACCUMULATION TO ATOMIC SWAPS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACCUMULATION: The process of buying and holding Bitcoin with the intention that its value will increase over time.
AIRDROP: The free distribution of new cryptocurrencies or tokens to holders of an existing cryptocurrency, often Bitcoin.
ALTCOIN: Any cryptocurrency that is not Bitcoin.
API (Application Programming Interface): A set of definitions and protocols used to build and integrate application software. In Bitcoin, APIs are used to interact with nodes and explore the blockchain.
ASIC (Application-Specific Integrated Circuit): Hardware designed specifically for mining Bitcoin, much more efficient than CPUs or GPUs.
ATOMIC SWAP: A direct exchange of one cryptocurrency for another without the need for an intermediary.
ATH (All-Time High): The highest price Bitcoin has ever reached in its history.

SECTION B - BEAR MARKET TO BULL MARKET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BEAR MARKET: A period of time when asset prices (including Bitcoin) are falling or are expected to fall.
BIP (Bitcoin Improvement Proposal): A design document that provides information to the Bitcoin community, or describes a new feature, process, or environment for Bitcoin. For example, BIP39 for seed phrases.
BITCOIN (BTC): The world's first and largest decentralized cryptocurrency. Also refers to the network and protocol.
BITCOIN CORE: The reference software implementation of a Bitcoin node, maintained by a group of developers. It is the foundation of the network.
BITCOIN MAXIMALIST: Someone who believes that Bitcoin is the only cryptocurrency that matters in the long term and that all others are inferior or unnecessary.
BLOCK: A file that permanently contains and records Bitcoin transaction data that is confirmed and added to the blockchain.
BLOCKCHAIN: A distributed, immutable, and decentralized database that records all Bitcoin transactions in a chain of cryptographically linked blocks.
BTC: The three-letter ticker symbol for Bitcoin.
BULL MARKET: A period of time when asset prices (including Bitcoin) are rising or are expected to rise.

SECTION C - CENSORSHIP TO CUSTODY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CENSORSHIP (Resistance to): Bitcoin's ability to resist interference or blocking of transactions by governments, corporations, or any centralized entity.
CODE AUDIT: A systematic review of a program's source code to find errors, security holes, or violations of programming rules. Crucial in the development of Bitcoin and related projects.
COLD STORAGE / COLD WALLET: A method of storing Bitcoin where the private keys are kept offline, which drastically reduces the risk of cyberattacks. Examples: hardware wallets, paper wallets.
COLLATERAL: Assets pledged as security for a loan or to back a position in a financial contract.
CONFIRMATION: A Bitcoin transaction is considered confirmed once it has been included in a block and that block has been added to the blockchain. The more confirmations, the more secure the transaction.
CONSENSUS ALGORITHM: A mechanism used by a distributed network to achieve agreement on the state of the ledger. In Bitcoin, this is Proof of Work.
CRYPTOGRAPHY: The study and practice of techniques for secure communication in the presence of adversaries. Fundamental to Bitcoin's security.
CUSTODY: The act of holding and securing financial assets on behalf of another person. Many exchanges offer custodial services, but self-custody is preferred by bitcoiners.

SECTION D - DAO TO DUSTING ATTACK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization): An organization governed by rules encoded in a smart contract, transparent and immutable, without a central authority. Not directly part of Bitcoin, but a relevant concept in the crypto space.
DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging): An investment strategy in which a fixed amount of money is invested regularly, regardless of the asset's price. Smoothes out market volatility over the long term.
DECENTRALIZATION: The property of a network or system where there is no single central authority. Bitcoin is decentralized, making it resistant to censorship and control.
DeFi (Decentralized Finance): A movement that seeks to recreate traditional financial services (lending, insurance, trading) using blockchain technology without intermediaries. Mostly on Ethereum and other altcoins, not directly on Bitcoin, although there are bridges and solutions like Liquid.
DUSTING ATTACK: Sending tiny amounts of Bitcoin (dust) to multiple addresses in the hope of tracking user activity across the network.

SECTION E - ECOSYSTEM TO EVANGELIST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BITCOIN ECOSYSTEM: The entire set of projects, companies, individuals, and technologies revolving around Bitcoin.
ELLIPTIC CURVE CRYPTOGRAPHY (ECC): A mathematical technique used in public key cryptography to generate public keys from private keys in Bitcoin.
EXCHANGE: A platform where users can buy, sell, or trade Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies.
BITCOIN EVANGELIST: A passionate person who promotes Bitcoin and its principles.

SECTION F - FAUCET TO FORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BITCOIN FAUCET: A website or application that distributes small amounts of Bitcoin for free to introduce new users to the world of cryptocurrencies.
FIAT: Government-issued fiat currency (e.g., dollar, euro). Bitcoin is an alternative to fiat money.
FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out): Fear of missing out on an investment opportunity, often leading to impulsive buying decisions.
FORK:
• SOFT FORK: A backward-compatible software update that introduces new, stricter rules. Old nodes can still validate, but new blocks will be invalid for them.
• HARD FORK: A backward-incompatible software update that requires all nodes to upgrade. Results in two separate chains if there is no total consensus.

SECTION G - GENESIS TO GOVERNANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
GENESIS BLOCK: The very first block in the Bitcoin blockchain, mined by Satoshi Nakamoto on January 3, 2009.
GOVERNANCE: The process by which decisions are made and implemented in a decentralized network like Bitcoin. It's a complex and ongoing process, without a central authority.

SECTION H - HALVING TO HOT WALLET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HALVING: A pre-programmed event in the Bitcoin network that halves the block reward received by miners. Occurs approximately every four years and reduces the issuance of new Bitcoin.
HASH: A mathematical function that takes an input of any size and produces a fixed-size output. Cryptographic hash functions are fundamental to Bitcoin's security.
HASH RATE: The amount of computing power being used to mine Bitcoin. The higher the hash rate, the more secure and decentralized the network.
HODL: A slang term in the crypto space, which originated from a typo of "hold." It means to hold Bitcoin for the long term, regardless of price volatility.
HOT WALLET: A Bitcoin wallet that is connected to the internet, making it convenient for quick transactions but more vulnerable to attacks. Examples: online or mobile software wallets.

SECTION I - IMMUTABLE TO INSTITUTIONAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
IMMUTABLE: The property of the Bitcoin blockchain that means once a transaction is recorded, it cannot be altered or deleted.
INFLATION: The general increase in prices and the fall in purchasing power of currency. Bitcoin is designed to be deflationary due to its limited supply.
INSTITUTIONAL INVESTOR: Large companies or investment funds that invest significant sums of capital in assets like Bitcoin.
INTEROPERABILITY: The ability of different systems and protocols to communicate and work together.

SECTION J - HIERARCHICAL DETERMINISTIC
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HIERARCHICAL DETERMINISTIC (HD WALLET): A type of wallet that generates multiple Bitcoin addresses from a single seed phrase, allowing for easy backup and management.

SECTION K - KILO TO KYC
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
KILO BITCOIN (kBTC): One thousand Bitcoin.
KYC (Know Your Customer): Processes that financial institutions and exchanges implement to verify the identity of their customers. Often criticized for its impact on privacy.

SECTION L - LIGHTNING TO LIQUID
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
LIGHTNING NETWORK: A Layer 2 scaling solution for Bitcoin that enables instant, low-cost off-chain transactions, improving scalability and privacy.
LIQUID NETWORK: A Bitcoin sidechain developed by Blockstream, which allows faster and more private Bitcoin transactions with pegged assets (LBTC).
LSP (Lightning Service Provider): A service provider on the Lightning Network that helps users connect and make payments, similar to an Internet service provider.

SECTION M - MEMPOOL TO MULTISIG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MEMPOOL (Memory Pool): A "waiting area" for Bitcoin transactions that have been broadcast to the network but have not yet been included in a block by a miner.
MERKLE TREE: A cryptographic data structure that allows for efficient verification of data integrity. Used in Bitcoin to summarize all transactions in a block into a single hash (Merkle Root).
MINING: The process of using computing power to solve cryptographic problems and add new blocks of transactions to the Bitcoin blockchain. This is how new Bitcoin are created and the network is secured.
MINING POOL: A group of miners who combine their computing power (hash rate) to increase their chances of finding a block and sharing the rewards.
MULTISIG: An address that requires multiple private keys to sign a transaction before the associated Bitcoin can be spent. Adds an extra layer of security.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION N - NODE TO NO-KYC
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
NODE: A computer that participates in the Bitcoin network by running the Bitcoin Core software. Nodes validate transactions and blocks, and broadcast information across the network.
FULL NODE: A node that downloads and verifies a complete copy of the entire Bitcoin blockchain, contributing to the network's security and decentralization.
NO-KYC: Operations or platforms that do not require users to go through Know Your Customer (KYC) processes, prioritizing privacy.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION O - OFF-CHAIN TO OTC
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OFF-CHAIN: Transactions that occur outside the main blockchain, such as those performed on the Lightning Network, to improve scalability and privacy.
ON-CHAIN: Transactions that occur and are recorded directly on the main Bitcoin blockchain.
ORDINAL THEORY: A system for numbering and tracking individual Satoshis on the Bitcoin blockchain, allowing for the creation of "inscriptions" (NFTs) directly on Bitcoin. A recent and controversial addition.
ORPHAN BLOCK: A successfully mined block that is not part of the main chain because another miner found a competing block at the same time.
OTC (Over-The-Counter): The trading of Bitcoin that occurs directly between two parties, without the intermediation of a public exchange. Often used for large volumes.

SECTION P - P2P TO PSEUDONYMOUS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
P2P (Peer-to-Peer): Refers to a network where participants interact directly with each other without a central intermediary. Bitcoin is a P2P network.
PAPER WALLET: A cold storage method where Bitcoin public and private keys are printed on paper. Less recommended today due to security and durability risks.
PRIVACY: The ability to conduct transactions without revealing personal identity. Although Bitcoin transactions are pseudonymous, they are not completely anonymous.
PROOF OF WORK (PoW): The consensus algorithm used by Bitcoin, where miners compete to solve a complex computational problem. It's costly but secures the network.
PROTOCOL: A set of rules that govern how systems and networks communicate. The Bitcoin Protocol is the set of rules that govern the network.
PSEUDONYMOUS: In Bitcoin, transactions are associated with addresses (pseudonyms), not directly with real identities, although blockchain analysis can attempt to deanonymize.
PUBLIC KEY CRYPTOGRAPHY: A system of cryptography that uses a pair of keys (one public and one private) for encryption and digital signature. In Bitcoin, it's used to sign transactions and verify ownership.
