Bitcoin Manual for the Unbanked

Participate in the global financial system without traditional banking

Why Use Bitcoin Without a Bank Account?

1
Global Accessibility

No bank account needed, just access to a mobile phone or computer

2
Low Transaction Costs

Sending money with Bitcoin is often cheaper than traditional remittance services

3
Censorship Resistance

No one can freeze or confiscate Bitcoin funds

4
Secure Savings

Protection against inflation and local currency devaluation

Use Cases

International Remittances

Send and receive money from anywhere in the world without intermediaries.

Example: A worker abroad sends Bitcoin to their family in their home country without paying high fees.
Peer-to-Peer Payments

Transfer Bitcoin directly and quickly from one user to another.

Example: A farmer sells their produce and receives Bitcoin on their mobile phone.
Savings & Inflation Protection

Hold Bitcoin as a store of value to protect against local currency devaluation.

Example: A worker converts their income into Bitcoin to preserve purchasing power.
Microwork & Remote Work

Receive payments in Bitcoin for online tasks or freelance jobs.

Example: A graphic designer in Venezuela works for international clients and gets paid in Bitcoin.

Bitcoin Circular Economy Manual

Build independent, censorship-resistant financial ecosystems

Fundamentals

What is a Bitcoin Circular Economy?

A system where people can earn, spend, and reinvest satoshis within a community, reducing dependence on fiat currencies.

Benefits
  • Censorship resistance
  • Low fees with Lightning Network
  • Global accessibility
  • Privacy and autonomy

Use Cases

Local Community
  • Farmers' markets accepting Bitcoin
  • Restaurants and businesses
  • Independent professionals
Financial Justice
  • Microloans between community members
  • Decentralized crowdfunding
  • Emergency funds and savings clubs
Digital Barter Systems
  • Product and service exchange
  • Bitcoin-only classified platforms
  • Community marketplaces
Instant Payments
  • Lightning Network payments
  • Digital tipping systems
  • Micropayment solutions

City/Neighborhood Implementation

1
Identify Local Ecosystem

Make a list of businesses, professionals, and entrepreneurs interested in accepting Bitcoin. Contact influential people in the community to educate them about Bitcoin.

2
Create Payment System

Implement Lightning-compatible wallets. Use Bitcoin POS like BTCPay Server, BlueWallet, or FEDI. Explain the importance of maintaining custody of private keys.

3
Educate the Community

Organize talks, meetups, and workshops about Bitcoin. Create social media content on how to use Bitcoin. Implement a digital newsletter on Nostr.

4
Implement Local Lightning Network

Encourage the creation of Lightning nodes among merchants. Establish liquidity channels between local businesses to reduce fees.

5
Build Marketplace

Use decentralized platforms like Plebeian Market or Nostr Classifieds. Create a bulletin board where businesses can offer their products for Bitcoin.
